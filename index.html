Calculator Notes

  • Boundary: Operational control of single data center asset (Scope 1 and 2 only; Scope 3 excluded)
  • No carbon offsets applied
Scope 1
  • Total Scope 1 = Diesel + Natural Gas + Refrigerant Leakage
  • Diesel Emissions (kg CO₂e) = Diesel (gallons) × 10.21
  • Natural Gas Emissions (kg CO₂e) = Natural Gas (therms) × 5.3
  • Refrigerant Emissions (kg CO₂e) = Refrigerant Charge (kg) × Leakage Rate (%) × GWP
  • Refrigerant GWPs based on IPCC AR5 100-year values
  • Due to significant variability across facilities and minor contribution to emissions, on-site fuel use for vehicles is excluded in Scope 1 calculation
  • Assume diesel consumption reflects both emergency and testing operations
  • Assume no seasonality impact on emissions
Scope 2
  • Total Scope 2 = IT MW × 8760 × PUE × Grid EF × (1 − Renewable%) ÷ 1000
  • 8760 = hours per year
  • Assume constant annual average PUE
  • Assume installed IT capacity operates at full load for the full year
  • Assume steady full-year operation
  • Scope 2 calculated using location-based grid emission factors from EPA eGRID
  • Assume renewable energy sources have zero Scope 2 emissions
  • Assume no battery storage used onsite for peak shaving or carbon shifting
  • Assume no seasonality impact on emissions
  • A PUE of 1.35 reflects the current industry benchmark for modern, well-operated hyperscale and institutional-grade colocation data centers; legacy enterprise facilities typically operate in the 1.6–2.0+ range, while best-in-class hyperscale campuses can achieve 1.1–1.2
  • Based on 2023 U.S. Energy Information Administration (EIA) data, the average grid emission factor for the United States is approximately 367 kg CO₂e/MWh
